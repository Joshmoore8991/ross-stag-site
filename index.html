    Essentials & Emergencies

    Save these before you fly. 112 works from any phone, no SIM, no credit, English operators.

    All Emergencies

    112

    Police, ambulance, fire. Free from any phone, English-speaking operators.

    Tourist Police

    +34 932 903 000

    Mossos d'Esquadra. Station on La Rambla 43, open 24h, English-speaking. Go here for denuncias (police reports for stolen phones/wallets).

    24h Pharmacy Nearby

    Farmàcia Cervera — Muntaner 254, ~10 min walk from the hotel.
    Farmàcia Clápes — La Rambla 98, 24/7 in the centre.

    Look for the flashing green cross — open pharmacies are labelled “farmacia de guardia”.

    Nearest ER

    Hospital Clínic — C/ Villarroel 170, ~15 min walk. 24h A&E, English staff on duty.

    Keep your GHIC/EHIC card on you. Bring passport.

    Lost Passport

    1. File a denuncia at La Rambla tourist police.
    2. Call the UK Consulate Barcelona: +34 933 666 200.
    3. Apply for Emergency Travel Document via gov.uk.

    Photograph your passport now and save it to your phone.

    Lost / Stolen Card

    Block it in your banking app first, then call. Barclays, Monzo, Starling & Revolut all freeze instantly in-app.

    For reporting theft to insurance you'll need the denuncia from the tourist police.

    Pickpocket Hotspots

    La Rambla, metro L3 green line, Barceloneta beach, Sagrada Família queue, Plaça Catalunya.

    Phone in front pocket, bag zipped across the chest, no wallet bulge. Don't flash cash at bars.

    Hotel Safe Tip

    Drop passports + one bank card in the room safe on arrival. Carry a photo of your passport, one card, and some cash. Losing everything in one go ruins a trip.

    Barcelona Scam Playbook

    Scammers work the Rambla, Gothic and metro in teams and they target lads in groups. Learn the plays once and you'll spot them sober or seven-deep.

    “Free Entry” Strip Clubs

    Hustlers on La Rambla wave flyers for “free champagne” clubs. Drinks are €30–€80 each, your card gets run for hundreds, and heavies block the door until you pay.

    Rule: if it's advertised on the street, walk past. Any club worth visiting doesn't need to drag you in.

    Petition / Clipboard Gang

    “Deaf&mute” crews outside Sagrada Família and on the Rambla shove a clipboard in your chest. While you read, a second crew lifts your phone or wallet.

    Rule: hands up, keep walking, don't engage. There is no real petition.

    Friendship Bracelet / Rosemary

    Someone grabs your wrist and ties on a bracelet, or shoves rosemary in your hand “for luck”. Then demands €5–€20 and kicks off if you refuse.

    Rule: never take anything handed to you on the street. If it's already on, pull it off and leave.

    Three-Card Monte

    Crowd around a cardboard box on the Rambla watching someone “win” at shells or cards. Every player is in on it. You'll never win, and pickpockets work the spectators.

    Rule: don't even stop to watch — that's the whole setup.

    Sauce / Bird-Poo Distraction

    Something warm hits your shoulder. A friendly local rushes over with tissues to help clean it off — and lifts your wallet while they do.

    Rule: if you get splattered, keep walking to the hotel or a shop bathroom. Do not let strangers touch you or your bag.

    Fake Plainclothes Police

    Two men flash a wallet badge and say they need to check your notes and cards for “counterfeits”. They're not police.

    Rule: real Mossos never check your wallet on the street. Ask for uniformed ID, say “comisaría” (station), and walk towards the nearest busy shop.

    Taxi “Meter Broken”

    Unofficial taxi, or a licensed driver claiming the meter is off. You get quoted €60 for a €15 hop.

    Rule: only black & yellow cabs with a licence number on the door. Insist on the meter (Tarifa 1 weekdays, Tarifa 2 nights/weekends). Airport to centre is a fixed €39–€45 flat rate.

    ATM Skim + Shoulder Surf

    Dodgy ATMs in tourist zones have skimmers, and “helpful” locals hover to read your PIN then pickpocket you a block later.

    Rule: only use ATMs inside a bank lobby (CaixaBank, BBVA, Santander). Cover the keypad. Ignore anyone offering help.

    Bar Bill & Round Skim

    Tourist-strip bars ring up drinks you didn't order or “couvert” charges for bread. Bills get worse the drunker the table looks.

    Rule: one lad checks the itemised bill before tapping card. “La cuenta detallada, por favor.” Tip in cash only — card tips often vanish.

    Golden rule: phones in front pockets, one card on you (not the wallet), passport in the hotel safe. If a stranger creates touch, noise, or a favour, assume it's a setup and move.

    Getting Around

    Barcelona metro is cheap, clean, and covers everywhere you need. Taxis are regulated. Don't rent a car.

    T-casual — Best Value

    ~€12.55 for 10 single journeys (metro + bus + tram). One card per person — not shareable.

    Buy from any metro machine (English menu). One journey = 75 min including transfers.

    Hola Barcelona Card

    Unlimited travel including airport metro. 48h ~€17.50, 72h ~€25.50.

    Worth it if you're doing airport + heavy metro use. Otherwise T-casual wins.

    Taxi Apps

    FreeNow and Cabify work in licensed black-&-yellow taxis. Uber operates but is patchier.

    Street-hailing is fine — green roof light = available. Always on the meter, card accepted in most.

    Last Metro

    Sun–Thu: midnight. Fri: 2am. Sat: runs 24h. After hours: night bus (N-lines) or taxi — €10–20 across town.

    • Gràcia bars — walk.

    Airport Metro (L9 Sud)

    Runs to both terminals, ~35 min to centre. Special airport fare (€5.70) — T-casual does NOT cover this leg. Hola Barcelona does.

    You've got a private transfer, but handy if someone flies out early or arrives late.

    E-scooters & Bikes

    Lime & Bolt e-scooters on every corner. Bicing is resident-only.

    Do not ride drunk. Barcelona police enforce €500+ fines and will confiscate the scooter.

    Walking

    Gràcia → Plaça Catalunya = 25 min flat. Most of the city you can walk, especially nice in the evening down Passeig de Gràcia past the Gaudí houses.

    Tipping & Etiquette

    Spain is low-tip. Nobody expects 20%.
    Tapas bar: round up to the next euro, or drop loose change.
    Sit-down dinner: 5–10% if service was good, or round to the nearest €5.
    Taxi: round up to the next euro.
    Hotel porter: €1–€2 per bag.
    Cash only — card tips usually never reach the staff.
    Dinner kicks off 21:00+; asking for the bill usually needs a hand-scribble mime.

    Tip: Spanish breakfast starts late. Before 9am you're mostly looking at cafés and bakeries, not sit-down brunch. If the crew wants eggs before 10, head for Can Dendê, Granja Petitbó, or Federal and be there when they open.

    ⚠️ Sunday landing + Monday shutdowns: plenty of traditional Spanish places close Sunday night and all day Monday. Bar Joan, Hofmann, Granja La Pallaresa and most market stalls are Sunday-closed. Can Dendê and Federal usually run 7 days — treat them as the Day-1 backbone.

    Restaurant Picks

    Heads up: Spanish family spots (Can Solé, Cerveceria Catalana) often close Sunday night or Monday. For Day 1 (Sun 3 May) default to El Nacional, Parking Pizza, Brunch & Cake or Besta — all open late and 7 days. Call ahead for anything traditional.

    Interactive Packing Checklist

    Hangover Kit

    Pack this. You will need it by Monday morning.

    01

    Ibuprofen & Paracetamol

    Alternate, don't double-up. Spanish farmacias sell both over the counter.

    02

    Electrolytes

    Dioralyte sachets or SOS tabs. One in a pint of water before bed saves a day.

    03

    Bottled Water

    Grab a big one at Dia or Carrefour on day 1. Pint glass by the bed every night.

    04

    Eye Drops

    Optrex or similar. 4am flights + shisha = bloodshot city. Fixes photos too.

    05

    Plasters & Blister Pads

    New shoes + 12-hour days = carnage. Compeed saves the day.

    06

    Deodorant & Mouthwash

    Travel sizes. Rescue kit for the 20-minute shower-and-go turnaround.

    07

    Imodium

    Aioli on everything + midnight kebabs. Pack it. Tell no one. Everyone will need it.

    08

    Factor 30+ Suncream

    May sun in Barcelona is deceptive. Pool-day burns ruin the next night.

    09

    Spare Charger + Plug

    EU 2-pin. One dead phone on a night out = one lost lad.

    10

    Vitamin C / Berocca

    Fizz it in water with breakfast. Placebo or not, it helps.

    11

    Flip-flops

    Hotel room, pool, beach, emergency walk home. Pack them.

    12

    Snacks for the Room

    Crisps, nuts, chocolate. 4am returns + closed minibar = despair.
